Bernhard Firner is a teaching professor at Rutgers University, where he teaches courses on computer architecture and machine learning. Prior to Rutgers, Bernhard worked at a small drone autonomy startup and was a member of the NVIDIA autonomous driving team for eight years. He was one of the initial members of the end-to-end driving group at NVIDIA, and was involved in the data collection, labelling, training, and testing systems, which resulted in several patents and widely cited papers.
Prior to those experiences, Bernhard worked on low-power wireless sensors during his PhD thesis at Rutgers University and at a startup that made environmental sensors for the laboratory animal services market. His sensors reached lifetimes of 20+ years while running on coin cell batteries. His first job after completing his undergraduate degree was writing safety-critical code at an avionics company. The rigor and attention to detail required in that position has influenced his approach to problems ever since then.
